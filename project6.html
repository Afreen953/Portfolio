EMPOWERING HEALTH THROUGH CLOUD: BUILDING A RESILIENT INFRASTRUCTURE FOR HUMAN HEALTH PROJECT

Objective:

Our mission was to build a sturdy backbone for Human Health Project (HHP), a global nonprofit dedicated to revolutionizing health outcomes through the power of shared information. We aimed to craft an infrastructure capable of supporting HHP's noble cause â€“ empowering individuals worldwide to take charge of their health journey by tapping into a wealth of community-driven health data.

Tools Used:

  • Public cloud giants like AWS and Azure were our playgrounds.

  • We danced with Linux servers in virtual environments, mastering the art of Linux scripting along the way.

  • Our trusty companions, Chef, Ansible, Salt Stack, and Terraform, kept our configurations in check.

  • Load balancers, firewalls, and LAMP/LEMP servers formed the core of our infrastructure dance floor.

  • Network wizards like iptables, HAProxy, and DNS sprinkled their magic for seamless connectivity.

  • GitHub served as our conductor for continuous integration and deployment symphonies.

  • We kept a close watch on our infrastructure's heartbeat with monitoring and reporting tools.

Chapters:

  • Performance Tuning and Monitoring:

    Fine-tuning our setup, we maintained peak performance and reliability, keeping a vigilant eye on metrics.
